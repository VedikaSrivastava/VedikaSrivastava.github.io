A Comparative Study of Style Transfer Models

Conducted a comprehensive comparison and analysis of four style transfer models—Neural Style Transfer (NST), Fast Style Transfer (FST), Color-based Artistic Style Transfer (CBST), and Deep Feature Rotation (DFR) for Multimodal Image Style Transfer.

View Details

Tech Stack/Skills: Computer Vision, Deep Learning and Neural Networks, Image Processing, Research and Analysis

Details:

  • Conducted a comprehensive comparison and analysis of four style transfer models—Neural Style Transfer (NST), Fast Style Transfer (FST), Color-based Artistic Style Transfer (CBST), and Deep Feature Rotation (DFR) for Multimodal Image Style Transfer.
  • Assessed the models based on various metrics, including human evaluation, technical evaluation metrics (FID, LPIPS, SSIM), and time taken for style transfer, to determine their effectiveness in generating high-quality stylized images.
  • Found that Neural Style Transfer (NST) excels in artistic quality and style fidelity, while Fast Style Transfer (FST) demonstrates faster inference times, making them strong contenders for style transfer applications.
  • Potential for Improvement: Identified Color-based Artistic Style Transfer (CBST) and Deep Feature Rotation (DFR) as models with potential for development and improvement in terms of technical quality, opening avenues for further research.
  • Provided valuable insights to guide researchers and practitioners in selecting appropriate style transfer models based on specific requirements, such as speed, artistic quality, or technical quality, and emphasized the need for future work in advancing the field of image transformation applications.
